Wild Amboli Expedition

Amboli is a hill station in south Maharashtra, India. At an altitude of 690 m it is the last hill station before the coastal highlands of Goa and a relatively unexplored one. Amboli lies in the Sahayadri Hills of Western India, one of the world's "Eco Hot-Spots" and it therefore abounds in a variety of fairly unique flora and fauna The misty forests of Amboli are a Home for many species of Reptiles, Amphibians, Birds, Animals, and Plants. The walking trails to Shirgaonkar Point, Mahadevgarh, through the fog give us immense opportunities to explore, enjoy the Flora Fauna of Amboli. Wildlife of Amboli includes many species of Reptiles like Malabar Pit vipers, Vine Snakes, Green Keelback, Shield Tails etc.
